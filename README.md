## 📌 Project Overview

This IRB-approved capstone study examined home-based pain management strategies among individuals living with Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and their relationship to healthcare utilization, self-efficacy, and quality of life outcomes.

Participants were recruited through digital and community-based outreach efforts, allowing the study to reach individuals beyond traditional clinical settings. The project explored how self-management practices influence healthcare use and identified factors associated with emergency department visits and hospitalizations.

## 📣 Recruitment Strategy

Participants were recruited through a multi-platform outreach campaign designed to engage individuals living with Sickle Cell Disease outside traditional healthcare settings.

| Platform           | Recruitment Method                                      |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Instagram          | Study flyers, stories, and community outreach           |

## 📊 Methods

### Study Design

Cross-sectional online survey study involving adults living with Sickle Cell Disease.

### Inclusion Criteria

* Confirmed Sickle Cell Disease diagnosis
* Age ≥ 18 years
* English-speaking
* Resident of Florida
* Ability to provide informed consent

### Key Measures

| Measure                    | Instrument                            |
| -------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Pain Intensity             | Visual Analog Scale (VAS)             |
| Self-Efficacy              | Adapted Stanford CDSMP Scale          |
| Quality of Life            | SF-12 Composite Score                 |
| Healthcare Utilization     | Emergency visits and hospitalizations |
| Pain Management Strategies | Structured checklist                  |

### Analysis

* Frequency distributions
* Descriptive statistics
* Cross-tabulation analyses
* Data visualization
* Healthcare utilization comparisons
* Quality assurance and outlier review

## 🔍 Key Findings

* Rest and over-the-counter medications were the most commonly reported pain management strategies.
* Higher self-efficacy scores were associated with lower healthcare utilization.
* Disease severity was the strongest predictor of emergency department visits and hospitalizations.
* Insurance status influenced healthcare utilization patterns.
* Social media recruitment successfully reached individuals who may not have been captured through clinic-based recruitment methods.
