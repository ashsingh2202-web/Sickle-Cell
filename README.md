📌 Project Overview
This IRB-approved capstone study examined home-based pain management strategies used by individuals living with Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) and their relationship to healthcare utilization and quality of life. Participants were recruited entirely through digital and social media channels, making this an online community-based participatory study reaching SCD patients beyond traditional clinical settings.
All data were collected via online survey, tracked and analyzed in Microsoft Excel, and visualized for reporting and presentation.
---
🎯 Research Questions
What home-based pain management strategies do individuals with SCD most commonly use?
Is self-efficacy associated with reduced emergency department visits and hospitalizations?
What demographic and clinical factors predict high healthcare utilization in this population?
Does pain management strategy diversity correlate with better quality of life outcomes?
---
📣 Recruitment Strategy
Participants were recruited through a multi-platform social media campaign designed to reach SCD patients outside of clinical settings:
Platform	Recruitment Method
Instagram	Study flyers posted as static images and stories; shared in SCD community accounts
Facebook	Flyers shared in SCD patient support groups and advocacy pages
YouTube	Short study explainer video describing the study, eligibility, and how to participate
Physical Flyers	Distributed in clinic waiting rooms and SCD community events
All digital materials were designed to be accessible, visually clear, and written at an appropriate health literacy level. Recruitment posts linked directly to the online consent form and survey.

📊 Methods
Study Design
Cross-sectional online survey study. Participants self-enrolled via social media recruitment and completed the survey on a secure platform.
Inclusion Criteria
Confirmed SCD diagnosis
Age ≥ 18 years
Able to provide informed consent in English
Living in Florida
Key Measures
Measure	Instrument	Scale
Pain intensity	Visual Analog Scale (VAS)	0–10
Self-efficacy	Stanford CDSMP Self-Efficacy Scale (adapted)	0–100
Quality of life	SF-12 composite score	0–100
Healthcare utilization	Self-report (ED visits, hospitalizations, missed work days)	Count
Pain management strategies	7-item binary checklist	0–7
Analysis (Microsoft Excel)
Frequency tables and percentage distributions for all categorical variables
Mean, median, and standard deviation for continuous variables
Pivot tables for cross-tabulation (strategy use by severity; utilization by insurance type)
Scatter plots and bar charts for visual reporting
Conditional formatting for data quality checks and outlier flagging
Full Excel workflow documented in `analysis/excel_analysis_guide.md`
---
🔍 Key Findings
Rest and OTC medications were the most frequently reported home-based strategies
Participants with higher self-efficacy scores reported fewer ED visits and hospitalizations
Disease severity was the strongest predictor of healthcare utilization
Uninsured participants showed higher ED visit rates, highlighting insurance as a key access barrier
Social media recruitment successfully reached participants outside clinical settings, including uninsured and underserved SCD patients unlikely to be captured through hospital-based recruitment
---
💡 Public Health Implications
Findings support integrating self-efficacy-building into SCD care coordination programs
Social media recruitment demonstrates feasibility for reaching hard-to-access populations in public health research
Insurance disparities in utilization highlight the need for case management for uninsured SCD patients
Results contribute to evidence supporting multimodal, patient-directed pain management in SCD guidelines
